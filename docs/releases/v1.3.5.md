# QueueLess — v1.3.5

**Codename: Nebula — Pulse**

## 📖 Overview

Nebula makes QueueLess proactive and self-managing: customers are alerted
before their turn, expired tokens can return, individual services pause
independently, staff work is attributed, and the queue resets itself
overnight.

Building on Eclipse's account management and priority engine, this release
automates the routine so the team can focus on serving.

## 🎯 Objectives

- Alert customers *before* they are called.
- Recover from expiries without losing customers.
- Give admins service-level (not just global) control.
- Attribute every action to the staff member who performed it.

## ✨ Added

- **Proactive push notifications** at position 2 ("almost up") and
  position 1 ("you're next"), working in background tabs.
- **Token re-queue** within 2 hours of issuance (preserves service,
  priority, group size).
- **Group / family tokens** (size 1–5) with badges.
- **Per-service pause/resume** — priority tokens always bypass.
- **SLA wait alerts** on the dashboard.
- **Staff performance metrics** (per-staff served / avg time via Mongo
  aggregation).
- **Daily scheduled auto-reset** (HH:MM, timezone-aware).
- **Multiple admin accounts** (up to 10, bcrypt-hashed).
- **Display-board welcome message**.
- **Appointment → priority-token auto-merge** (±5 min window).

## 🐛 Fixed

- Staff actions correctly attributed (`staffUsername` threading, both
  portals).
- Temporal-dead-zone crash on the customer token page.
- Analytics cross-reference RTDB so *all* historical tokens count.

## 🏛️ Architecture Progress

Nebula introduces the scheduled-services pattern (`setInterval` sweepers
for reset and appointment merge) and staff attribution on the analytics
event log — the basis of Polaris's per-counter analytics.

Known limitations at this release:

- Single fixed industry service list (custom queues arrive in Polaris).
